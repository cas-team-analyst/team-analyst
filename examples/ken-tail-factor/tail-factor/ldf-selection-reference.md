# LDF selection: triangle diagnostic reference

Source: *LDF Selection Reference* (triangle diagnostics for interpreting development patterns and guiding loss development factor selection). Use with paid, incurred, count triangles, and link-ratio diagnostics—not a substitute for actuarial judgment or engagement-specific facts.

---

## Single-scenario signals and implications

Each block lists typical **signals** (what to look for) and **implication for LDF selection** (directional guidance).

### Catastrophic claims

- **Signals:** Large spike in incremental loss for one origin/development age; severity (loss/claim) jump not matched by counts; cumulative loss % jumps vs. history.
- **Implication:** Exclude or cap CAT diagonals; use unaffected years.

### Decreased case reserve adequacy

- **Signals:** Paid catching up to incurred faster than history; case reserve per open claim drops sharply; incurred link ratios lower than baseline.
- **Implication:** Raise paid LDFs or lower incurred LDFs.

### Increased case reserve adequacy

- **Signals:** Incurred rises faster than paid in early ages; case reserve per open claim increasing; incurred link ratios above baseline.
- **Implication:** Lower paid LDFs or raise incurred LDFs.

### Faster reporting rates

- **Signals:** Higher % of ultimate reported at 12/24m; count link ratios early are below history; early cumulative loss % also higher.
- **Implication:** Reduce early-age LDFs; shorten tail.

### Slower reporting rates

- **Signals:** Lower % of ultimate reported at 12/24m; count link ratios early are above history; loss cumulative lags history.
- **Implication:** Raise early-age LDFs; lengthen tail.

### Growing book of business

- **Signals:** Higher total counts for recent origins; higher early cumulative losses; incremental counts proportionally higher across ages.
- **Implication:** Weight recent years more; exposure-adjust.

### Shrinking book of business

- **Signals:** Lower total counts for recent origins; lower cumulative losses at maturity; smaller incremental counts across ages.
- **Implication:** Adjust for credibility; avoid over-weighting small years.

### Increased settlement rates

- **Signals:** Closed % of reported higher early; paid link ratios at later ages below history; case reserves drop faster than usual.
- **Implication:** Reduce late-age LDFs; shorten tail.

### Decreased settlement rates

- **Signals:** Closed % of reported lower early; paid link ratios at later ages above history; case reserves flat or rising late.
- **Implication:** Raise late-age LDFs; extend tail.

### Changing claim severity trend

- **Signals:** Severity triangle trending up or down by origin; loss link ratios diverge from count link ratios; incremental severity changes consistent across ages.
- **Implication:** Adjust loss LDFs separately from counts.

### Inflation or superimposed inflation

- **Signals:** Calendar year loss growth > count growth; larger incremental paid in recent calendar years; severity trend positive in diagnostics.
- **Implication:** Raise loss LDFs; possibly adjust for inflation.

### Benefit level changes / legislative reform

- **Signals:** Step-change in severity post-effective date; count or closure patterns shift; link ratios change structurally after law change.
- **Implication:** Segment pre/post; separate LDFs.

### Operational change in claims handling

- **Signals:** Step-change in closure or reporting rates; link ratios shift after change date; severity pattern change across origins.
- **Implication:** Select factors from post-change years.

### Reopened claims increase

- **Signals:** Late-age count increments spike; late-age loss increments spike; paid link ratios at late ages above history.
- **Implication:** Increase late-age LDFs; longer tail.

### Change in reinsurance attachment/retention

- **Signals:** Drop in large losses/severity without count change; severity step-change; paid increments for large claims reduced.
- **Implication:** Lower loss LDFs; adjust severity end.

### Mix of business shift

- **Signals:** Change in severity patterns; change in closure or reporting rates by origin; counts/losses by claim type shift.
- **Implication:** Segment and select LDFs separately.

### Frequency shock (non-CAT)

- **Signals:** Count increment spike at early ages; severity stable; loss increments rise in line with counts.
- **Implication:** Remove or treat separately in averages.

### Data system change / restatement

- **Signals:** Step-change in both loss and count patterns; discontinuity in link ratios; same-age changes across origins.
- **Implication:** Correct or exclude impacted years.

### Bulk reserve release or strengthening

- **Signals:** Incremental incurred spike or drop across origins; case reserve per open changes sharply; incurred link ratios distorted.
- **Implication:** Exclude impacted diagonals or restate.

### Exposure base change

- **Signals:** Counts and losses shift proportionally across ages; change aligns with exposure measure shift; severity stable.
- **Implication:** Normalize or segment triangles.

## Combined scenarios

When multiple patterns appear together, treat interactions explicitly.

| Combined pattern | Typical signals | LDF selection note |
| --- | --- | --- |
| CAT losses + increased case reserve adequacy | Loss increment spike (CAT); incurred > paid gap widens; case per open rises post-CAT | Use unaffected paid; adjust incurred cautiously |
| Slower reporting + decreased settlement | Lower early % reported; higher early count link ratios; low closure % of reported | Higher early and late LDFs; longer tail |
| Growing book + faster reporting | Higher total counts in recent years; higher early % reported; lower early count link ratios | Reduce early LDFs but adjust for volume |
| Shrinking book + increased settlement | Lower total counts in recent years; higher early closure %; lower late-age paid link ratios | Shorten tail; adjust for low credibility |
| Legislative change + severity trend shift | Severity step-change post-law; ongoing severity trend in same direction; count/closure patterns stable | Segment pre/post; adjust loss LDFs for trend |

## Differentiating similar scenarios

Use **counts vs. losses**, **timing** (early vs. late development), and **paid vs. incurred vs. case** to separate drivers.

### Reporting vs. settlement pace

| Topic | Slower reporting | Decreased settlement |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Counts | Reported counts lag prior patterns at early maturities | Reported counts normal but closure % lags historical |
| Case | Case reserves inflated at early maturities | Case reserves remain higher at later maturities |
| Paid/incurred | Both paid and incurred slower to emerge | Paid slower to emerge while incurred may be on track |

| Topic | Faster reporting | Increased settlement |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Counts | Reported counts spike early compared to prior origins | Closure % spikes early without unusual counts trend |
| Case | Case reserves drop quickly (more claims reported early) | Case reserves drop from faster payments, not reporting change |
| Paid/incurred | Paid and incurred emerge more quickly early | Paid spikes early; incurred relatively stable |

### Severity vs. frequency shocks

| Topic | Catastrophic claims | Frequency shock (non-CAT) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Counts vs. loss | Spike in severity without matching count increase | Proportional increases in counts and losses |
| Scope | Loss spike isolated to one or two development ages | Impact persists across multiple development ages |
| Pattern | Counts flat while losses surge | Higher counts for affected origin periods |

### Book size vs. other drivers

| Topic | Growing book | Shrinking book + increased settlement |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Volume | Counts and losses increase steadily in recent origins | Counts and losses decreasing over time |
| Credibility | Later origins have greater volume and more credibility | Less credibility in later origins due to smaller volume |
| Pattern | Consistent maturity patterns despite higher volume | Closure % higher early in development |

### Case adequacy and mixed drivers

| Topic | Increased case reserve adequacy | Decreased case reserve adequacy |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Incurred vs. paid | Widening gap (incurred vs. paid) | Narrowing gap |
| Case per open | Increases over time | Decreases over time |
| Link ratios | Incurred link ratios inflated relative to paid | Incurred suppressed relative to paid |

**Slower reporting + decreased settlement:** Both reported counts and closure % lag norms; paid slow to emerge; incurred also slow; case reserves stay high throughout.

**Slower reporting + increased case reserve adequacy:** Reported counts on track but incurred ≫ paid; severity inflation without count change; gap most pronounced.

**Legislative change + severity shift vs. inflation:** Legislation shows an **abrupt** step-change at certain origin years and may affect counts; **inflation** is gradual upward drift across maturities with counts often unaffected and smaller per-year impact that cumulates.

## Context elements for agentic LDF selection

When automating or structuring judgment, record these dimensions so adjustments are traceable.

| Context element | Description | Purpose for LDF selection |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Triangle type | Paid, incurred, reported counts, incremental, link ratios, or diagnostics | Identify which view the signal comes from to target the LDF adjustment |
| Maturity/age | Months or years since origin period start | Focus analysis on maturities most affected by the observed pattern |
| Signal direction | Above/below average; increasing/decreasing; widening vs. narrowing gaps | Decide whether to increase or decrease LDFs |
| Magnitude | Percent or dollar difference from historical average or median | Quantify how large an adjustment may need to be |
| Persistence | Single-age spike vs. multiple ages vs. trend over several origins | Short-term tweak vs. sustained factor change |
| Volatility | Year-to-year variability in the signal | How much to rely on recent data in selection |
| Correlation | Signal in paid and incurred, or counts and losses | Higher confidence when multiple views align |
| Book volume change | Shift in exposure or claim counts in recent origins | Credibility of recent maturities in selection |
| External events | CAT, legislation, operational shifts | Interpret signals in context; avoid over-adjustment |
| Reserve adequacy | Trend in gap between incurred and paid | Adjust incurred LDFs when adequacy is changing |
