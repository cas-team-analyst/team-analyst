# Triangle diagnostic scenarios (single-factor)

Condensed from **Method Selection Reference** (see [Method-Selection-Reference.pdf](Method-Selection-Reference.pdf)). Use these **signals** when reviewing triangles, LDF picks, and **method weights** (e.g. paid vs incurred chain ladder vs BF vs initial expected) in `origin_blends`.

Each scenario lists typical **diagnostic signals** and **implications for LDF selection** (not a substitute for engagement-specific judgment).

## Catastrophic claims

- Large spike in incremental loss for one origin / development age.
- Severity (loss per claim) jump not matched by counts.
- Cumulative loss % jumps vs. history.

**LDF implication:** Exclude or cap CAT diagonals; use unaffected years.

## Decreased case reserve adequacy

- Paid catching up to incurred faster than history.
- Case reserve per open claim drops sharply.
- Incurred link ratios lower than baseline.

**LDF implication:** Raise paid LDFs or lower incurred LDFs.

## Increased case reserve adequacy

- Incurred rises faster than paid in early ages.
- Case reserve per open claim increasing.
- Incurred link ratios above baseline.

**LDF implication:** Lower paid LDFs or raise incurred LDFs.

## Faster reporting rates

- Higher % of ultimate reported at 12 / 24 months.
- Count link ratios early are below history.
- Early cumulative loss % also higher.

**LDF implication:** Reduce early-age LDFs; shorten tail.

## Slower reporting rates

- Lower % of ultimate reported at 12 / 24 months.
- Count link ratios early are above history.
- Loss cumulative lags history.

**LDF implication:** Raise early-age LDFs; lengthen tail.

## Growing book of business

- Higher total counts for recent origins.
- Higher early cumulative losses.
- Incremental counts proportionally higher across ages.

**LDF implication:** Weight recent years more; exposure-adjust.

## Shrinking book of business

- Lower total counts for recent origins.
- Lower cumulative losses at maturity.
- Smaller incremental counts across ages.

**LDF implication:** Adjust for credibility; avoid over-weighting small years.

## Increased settlement rates

- Closed % of reported higher early.
- Paid link ratios at later ages below history.
- Case reserves drop faster than usual.

**LDF implication:** Reduce late-age LDFs; shorten tail.

## Decreased settlement rates

- Closed % of reported lower early.
- Paid link ratios at later ages above history.
- Case reserves flat or rising late.

**LDF implication:** Raise late-age LDFs; extend tail.

## Additional scenarios (page 2)

### Changing claim severity trend

- Severity triangle trending up or down by origin.
- Loss link ratios diverge from count link ratios.
- Incremental severity changes consistent across ages.

**LDF implication:** Adjust loss LDFs separately from counts.

### Inflation or superimposed inflation

- Calendar-year loss growth greater than count growth.
- Larger incremental paid in recent calendar years.
- Severity trend positive in diagnostics.

**LDF implication:** Raise loss LDFs; possibly adjust for inflation.

### Benefit level changes / legislative reform

- Step-change in severity post-effective date.
- Count or closure patterns shift.
- Link ratios change structurally after law change.

**LDF implication:** Segment pre/post; separate LDFs.

### Operational change in claims handling

- Step-change in closure or reporting rates.
- Link ratios shift after change date.
- Severity pattern change across origins.

**LDF implication:** Select factors from post-change years.

### Reopened claims increase

- Late-age count increments spike.
- Late-age loss increments spike.
- Paid link ratios late ages above history.

**LDF implication:** Increase late-age LDFs; longer tail.

### Change in reinsurance attachment / retention

- Drop in large losses / severity without count change.
- Severity step-change.
- Paid increments for large claims reduced.

**LDF implication:** Lower loss LDFs; adjust severity tail.

### Mix of business shift

- Change in severity patterns.
- Change in closure or reporting rates by origin.
- Counts / losses by claim type shift.

**LDF implication:** Segment and select LDFs separately.

### Frequency shock (non-CAT)

- Count increment spike at early ages.
- Severity stable.
- Loss increments rise in line with counts.

**LDF implication:** Remove or treat separately in averages.

### Data system change / restatement

- Step-change in both loss and count patterns.
- Discontinuity in link ratios.
- Same-age changes across origins.

**LDF implication:** Correct or exclude impacted years.

### Bulk reserve release or strengthening

- Incremental incurred spike or drop across origins.
- Case reserve per open changes sharply.
- Incurred link ratios distorted.

**LDF implication:** Exclude impacted diagonals or restate.

### Exposure base change

- Counts and losses shift proportionally across ages.
- Change aligns with exposure measure shift.
- Severity stable.

**LDF implication:** Normalize or segment triangles.
